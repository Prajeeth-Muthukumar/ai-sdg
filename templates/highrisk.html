High Risk.
Doctor conusltation is Required Immediately

1. Medical Care & Regular Checkups Consult a doctor: Get a proper diagnosis and follow medical advice strictly. Monitor health parameters: Regularly check blood pressure, sugar levels, cholesterol, etc. Take prescribed medications: Never skip prescribed medications and follow up with doctors as needed. Consider specialist care: If she has conditions like diabetes, heart disease, or other risks, consult a specialist.
2. Nutrition & Diet Eat a balanced diet: Include fresh fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Reduce processed food: Cut down on sugar, refined carbs, and trans fats. Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Follow specific dietary guidelines: If she has a condition like diabetes or hypertension, follow a diet plan tailored to her needs.
3. Physical Activity & Exercise Engage in regular exercise: Walking, yoga, or low-impact exercises help improve heart health. Strength training: Helps maintain bone and muscle strength, especially in older mothers. Avoid long periods of inactivity: If sitting for long hours, take breaks to move around.
4. Mental & Emotional Well-being Manage stress: Practice deep breathing, meditation, or mindfulness techniques. Seek social support: Stay connected with family and friends for emotional well-being. Consider counseling: If dealing with anxiety, depression, or emotional distress, professional therapy can help.
5. Sleep & Rest Prioritize good sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night. Create a bedtime routine: Reduce screen time and follow a relaxing routine before sleeping.
6. Avoid Harmful Habits Quit smoking & alcohol: If applicable, reducing or stopping these can improve overall health. Limit caffeine intake: Too much caffeine can affect sleep and increase anxiety.
7. Preventive Health Measures Get vaccinations: Stay updated with necessary vaccines like flu shots and others recommended by the doctor. Regular screenings: Get checked for conditions like osteoporosis, cancer, or heart disease as advised by doctors.
